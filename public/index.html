The Spectator views on Turing Test

In this Unit, we were listening to someone’s project Presentation and gave a spectator view on that topic. I have listened to the Turing test and its purpose because it is related to machines and its imitations. At first I had no idea about this topic, after their explanations I understood that Alan Turing introduced this concept to test whether a machine could imitate human intelligence well. This topic made us change our view on Artificial Intelligence.I liked the way of explaining the difference between intelligence and imitation. We realized that AI responses that seem very natural like a human, making it difficult to users whether it is man or machine. However, we have to understand that producing human-like answers does not mean AI truly thinks like humans do. It can also simply respond based on the prompt and patterns.My thought on this topic was that the imitations of these AI tools are only based on the given prompt or text. If the text is like a human, it will respond like a human otherwise it may also respond like a machine. This was my view on this topic. I’m not sure that my thought aligned with this but, somehow it worked along with that. Some of the technical terms that I have not been able to follow like witness rate and pass rate. They could have done very well if they explained the case study more approachable to the audience. I personally did not get it. Otherwise It was a fantastic topic to note and explore. Through this activity I learned just hearing something is not going to remain forever. we have to make a dive in it. Hope you enjoyed my post :)

How Projects Improved Me to Communicate Information?

In this unit, we were working on individual projects which helped us to understand the true importance of communication in learning and development. This project was not simply based on rubrics; Instead it was based on how we can communicate information, efforts that we are putting on it and self-learning. We chose to work on a chatbot and a dashboard project, which required us to explore, research and solve challenges that we had faced in those projects. During the chatbot project, communication played an important role because it helped me to organize ideas and  present information clearly. A chatbot mainly designed to communicate with users effectively, so that I understood how users can expect answers to those questions they ask. and also I made sure the responses were understandable and meaningful and  useful. This helped me think from the user’s perspective and I understood  the way information is shared clearly and effectively. Similarly, while working on the dashboard project, I’ve learnt how we can communicate information through different types of charts and graphs. This project insists us to make reliable and communicable graphs and charts which are taken from the complex data. So we can learn how to communicate complex information in an easy way. The main challenge I faced was organizing these conversations in chatbots. It seems very hard, after some tries, I Improved to make such conversations very reliable to the users. Some of my group members Aiswarya, Deepavarsini helped me to overcome some problems.

Why failure matters!!

Life is all about learning and growing from the mistake that we done.We are assigned to two things, one is about the case study of Therac-25 and the other is the book named “The right kind of wrong - The science of failing well”. Learning these topics was quite challenging because both the sources are in academic style and  it contains technical details about software errors and system failures. For me, it was not fully a new topic, since I had an idea about human error and some system design, but the depth of the case study was so hard to follow. Then we searched for some video resources about it. It became more understandable when we visually looked at some issues. Somehow I was able to connect the idea of human error to real-life situations. For example, we repeatedly make some mistakes while using software, but in the case of Therac-25, mistakes like that combined with poor design led to some unimaginable consequences. It made me realize that designing systems in software is important. We found that breaking that topic into smaller parts made it easier to understand the concepts clearly. Instead of reading everything, we divided the topics on equal weightage and explained our topics to each other and it was more approachable because we thought that it will be easy for us also and understanding concepts from people is also a good thing.From this chapter and the labwork, we felt that students who read everything assigned to the group are a failure. So we did it smartly by dividing the work. So I consider it as a growth from a failure. Overall, this experience was so cool and this showed me how failure is the most important thing in everyone’s life because getting everything without a failure leads to another failure.So, I want to end this by my own quote, “ Do mistakes and grow up from that”. I think this is the real goal of this chapter. Hope you enjoyed reading it
